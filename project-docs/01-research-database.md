# "Are You a Heretic?" — Research Database

## Purpose
This document contains the complete research on Christian doctrinal controversies, councils, condemnations, and the shifting nature of orthodoxy. It serves as the canonical data source for the quiz content.

## SECTION 1: THE SEVEN ECUMENICAL COUNCILS

### 1. First Council of Nicaea (325 AD)
- **Heresy condemned:** Arianism
- **Plain language:** Jesus was God's first creation — divine-ish but not eternal, not equal to the Father
- **Condemned figure:** Arius, Alexandrian priest
- **Council's position:** Christ is "of one substance (homoousios) with the Father" — fully divine, co-eternal, uncreated. "Begotten, not made."
- **Relatable question:** If someone asked you "Was there ever a time when Jesus didn't exist?", what would you say?
- **Modern trap:** Many Christians instinctively think of Jesus as "God's Son" in a way that implies he came *after* the Father — a created being who is subordinate. That's Arianism.
- **CRITICAL CONTEXT:** Nicaea's authority was almost immediately challenged. Within 10 years, anti-Nicene councils began overturning its decisions. For most of the 4th century (roughly 335–380), the Nicene position was the *minority* view, and homoousios was condemned by more councils than affirmed it. See SECTION 1B below.

### 2. First Council of Constantinople (381 AD)
- **Heresy condemned:** Macedonianism / Pneumatomachi ("Spirit-fighters"); also all forms of Arianism, Semi-Arianism, and homoianism
- **Plain language:** The Holy Spirit is a created force, not fully God — a kind of divine energy but not a person equal to Father and Son
- **Condemned figure:** Macedonius I of Constantinople; also all anti-Nicene factions
- **Council's position:** The Spirit is "the Lord, the Giver of Life, who proceeds from the Father, who with the Father and the Son is worshipped and glorified." Adopted the Cappadocian formula: one ousia (substance), three hypostaseis (persons).
- **Relatable question:** Is the Holy Spirit a person or a force/power?
- **Modern trap:** Many Christians functionally treat the Spirit as God's "energy" or "power" rather than a co-equal divine person. This is exactly what Constantinople condemned.
- **Also condemned:** Apollinarianism (Christ had no human rational mind — the Logos replaced it)
- **CRITICAL CONTEXT:** Constantinople didn't just affirm Nicaea — it *reversed 40+ years of anti-Nicene dominance*. This council was called by Emperor Theodosius I specifically to restore homoousios after it had been officially rejected by the imperial church since ~360. The formula "three persons, one God" was only stabilized HERE — and it immediately condemned everything the church had officially taught for the previous two decades. See SECTION 1B below.

### 3. Council of Ephesus (431 AD)
- **Heresy condemned:** Nestorianism
- **Plain language:** Jesus was basically two persons sharing one body — the human Jesus and the divine Word were loosely connected but separate
- **Condemned figure:** Nestorius, Patriarch of Constantinople
- **Council's position:** Christ is one person with two natures. Mary is rightly called "Theotokos" (God-bearer) because the person she bore was fully God
- **Relatable question:** When baby Jesus was lying in the manger, was God lying in that manger?
- **Modern trap:** Protestants who are uncomfortable calling Mary "Mother of God" often unwittingly adopt Nestorian logic. Also, anyone who says "the human part of Jesus did X but the divine part did Y" is splitting Christ into two persons.

### 4. Council of Chalcedon (451 AD)
- **Heresy condemned:** Monophysitism / Eutychianism
- **Plain language:** After the incarnation, Jesus had only ONE blended nature — his humanity was absorbed into his divinity like a drop of honey dissolved in the ocean
- **Condemned figure:** Eutyches, Dioscorus of Alexandria
- **Council's position:** Christ has TWO complete natures — divine and human — "without confusion, without change, without division, without separation"
- **Relatable question:** When Jesus was hungry, tired, or afraid — was that real, or was it just God pretending?
- **Modern trap:** Churches that emphasize Christ's power so heavily that his genuine human limitations seem like an act are functionally Monophysite.

### 5. Second Council of Constantinople (553 AD)
- **Heresy condemned:** "Three Chapters" — writings with Nestorian tendencies
- **Condemned figures:** Theodore of Mopsuestia, Theodoret, Ibas
- **Also condemned:** Origen's teachings (posthumously, ~300 years after his death) — including the pre-existence of souls and universal reconciliation (apokatastasis)
- **Modern trap:** If you believe all people will eventually be saved (universal reconciliation), this council condemned that view. If you believe in the pre-existence of souls, same.

### 6. Third Council of Constantinople (680-681 AD)
- **Heresy condemned:** Monothelitism
- **Plain language:** Christ had only one will (the divine will). His human will was either absent or completely overridden
- **Condemned figure:** Monothelites, including Pope Honorius I (a Pope condemned as a heretic by an ecumenical council!)
- **Council's position:** Christ has two wills — divine and human — and his human will freely submits to the divine
- **Relatable question:** When Jesus prayed "not my will but yours be done" in Gethsemane — was there a real "my will" that was different from the Father's?
- **Modern trap:** Anyone who thinks Jesus couldn't have *actually* chosen to sin (because "he's God, obviously") is close to Monothelitism. If his human will wasn't genuine, the temptations weren't real.

### 7. Second Council of Nicaea (787 AD)
- **Heresy condemned:** Iconoclasm
- **Plain language:** Religious images of Christ, Mary, and the saints are idolatry and should be destroyed
- **Council's position:** Icons may receive "veneration" (honor that passes through to what they depict) but not "worship" (which belongs to God alone). The incarnation itself validates material depiction of the divine.
- **Relatable question:** Is having a cross on your wall, a painting of Jesus, or a nativity scene a form of idolatry?
- **Modern trap:** Most Protestants are functional iconoclasts — stripped churches, no images, suspicion of "graven images." The Second Council of Nicaea says that's the heresy.

## SECTION 1B: THE ANTI-NICENE COUNCILS (325–381 AD)

*"The whole world groaned and was astonished to find itself Arian."* — Jerome, Dialogue Against the Luciferians

### Why This Matters for the Quiz
Between Nicaea (325) and Constantinople (381), the anti-Nicene faction dominated the institutional church through a series of councils that *outnumbered* the pro-Nicene ones. The Nicene Creed's key term — homoousios ("of one substance") — was officially rejected by the majority of councils in this period. For roughly 20 years (360–380), the imperial church's official doctrine was that the Son is merely "like" the Father, with no shared substance. Anyone affirming "three persons in one God" would have been condemned by the dominant councils of the age. **This is the period the quiz was missing.**

### The Theological Spectrum (4th Century)

| Position | Greek Term | Meaning | Who Held It |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Homoousian** (Nicene) | homoousios | Same substance — Father and Son share identical essence | Athanasius, Cappadocians, eventually Constantinople 381 |
| **Homoiousian** (Semi-Arian) | homoiousios | Similar substance — the Son's essence is like but not identical to the Father's | Basil of Ancyra, Council of Ancyra (358) |
| **Homoian** (Late compromise) | homoios | "Like" — no substance language at all; deliberately vague | Councils of Rimini, Seleucia, Constantinople 360 |
| **Anomoean** (Radical Arian) | anomoios | Unlike — the Son is fundamentally different from the Father | Aëtius of Antioch, Eunomius |

### The Anti-Nicene Councils

#### Council of Tyre (335 AD)
- **What happened:** Condemned Athanasius (Nicaea's champion) and effectively rehabilitated Arius
- **Position:** Anti-Nicene (political, not formally creedal)
- **Imperial backing:** Constantine I, seeking unity over theology
- **Why it matters:** First major reversal of Nicaea's authority, only 10 years after the council

#### Council of Antioch / "Dedication Council" (341 AD)
- **What happened:** ~90 bishops produced four replacement creeds that explicitly rejected homoousios
- **Position:** Homoian/Semi-Arian — the Son is "like" the Father but they conspicuously omitted shared substance language
- **Imperial backing:** Emperor Constantius I
- **Key fact:** No Western/Latin bishops were present. First large-scale creedal replacement of Nicaea
- **Condemned:** The Sabellian interpretation of homoousios (that it collapses three persons into one)

#### Council of Sardica (343 AD)
- **What happened:** East-West split. ~180 Western bishops affirmed Athanasius; ~180 Eastern bishops held a counter-synod at Philippopolis and anathematized the Western decisions
- **Position:** First formal East-West schism in Christian history. The Eastern counter-synod was anti-Nicene
- **Imperial backing:** Split — Constans I (West, pro-Nicene) vs Constantius II (East, anti-Nicene)
- **Why it matters:** Demonstrated that the church was structurally divided over Nicaea

#### Council of Arles (353 AD)
- **What happened:** 300+ bishops condemned Athanasius again, under direct imperial pressure
- **Position:** Anti-Nicene
- **Imperial backing:** Constantius II personally oversaw the proceedings
- **Why it matters:** The anti-Nicene project moved into the West

#### Council of Milan (355 AD)
- **What happened:** 300+ bishops forced to sign anti-Nicene formulas. Those who refused were immediately exiled: Pope Liberius, Hosius of Córdoba, Hilary of Poitiers
- **Position:** Anti-Nicene — homoousios explicitly rejected
- **Imperial backing:** Constantius II was physically present with armed guards
- **Key fact:** A Pope was exiled for defending the Nicene position. Only three significant bishops resisted. This may be the moment Jerome was describing.

#### Councils of Sirmium (347, 351, 357, 358, 359 AD)
Five councils at Sirmium tracked the full range of anti-Nicene theology:
- **Sirmium II (351):** Condemned homoousios and any substance terminology
- **Sirmium III (357) — "The Blasphemy of Sirmium":** The high point of radical Arianism. Condemned both homoousios AND homoiousios. The Father is "greater" and "more powerful" than the Son. Hilary of Poitiers gave it this nickname.
- **Sirmium IV / Council of Ancyra (358):** Homoiousian reaction — the Son is "similar in substance" (homoiousios). Rejected both Nicene homoousios and anomoean extremism.
- **Sirmium V (359):** Produced the homoian compromise formula — "the Son is like the Father according to the Scriptures" — which became the template for the twin councils of 359.
- **Key insight:** Shows the anti-Nicene party was NOT monolithic — they split into mutually hostile factions (homoiousian, homoian, anomoean)

#### Council of Rimini (359 AD) — THE BIG ONE
- **What happened:** ~400 Western bishops initially voted *for* the Nicene Creed. Then imperial envoys forced them to reverse their decision and sign a homoian formula before they were allowed to leave.
- **Position:** Homoian (coerced) — "the Son is like the Father according to the Scriptures"
- **Imperial backing:** Absolute — Constantius II used detention, delay, and threats to coerce compliance
- **Why it matters:** The largest council of the era. A pro-Nicene majority was physically coerced into signing anti-Nicene documents. This is the clearest evidence that the anti-Nicene "consensus" was manufactured by imperial force.

#### Council of Seleucia (359 AD)
- **What happened:** ~160 Eastern bishops held the companion council to Rimini. Homoiousian majority eventually signed the same homoian compromise.
- **Position:** Divided — homoiousian majority, but ultimately signed the homoian formula
- **Imperial backing:** Constantius II coordinated both councils

#### Council of Constantinople (360 AD) — ANTI-NICENE APEX
- **What happened:** Standardized the homoian creed as the empire-wide doctrinal standard. Condemned both homoousios AND homoiousios as "unbiblical."
- **Position:** Pure homoian — the Son is "like" the Father, no substance language permitted
- **Imperial backing:** Total — Constantius II enforced this as the official faith of the Roman Empire
- **Duration:** This remained the imperial orthodoxy for 20 years (360–380)
- **Key fact:** For two decades, "three persons in one God" was officially heretical. The Nicene party was underground.

### The Reversal: How Nicaea Won

The anti-Nicene dominance collapsed due to:
1. **Internal fracture:** Homoiousians, homoians, and anomoeans couldn't agree on anything except that homoousios was wrong
2. **Death of Constantius II (361):** Without imperial enforcement, the artificial consensus crumbled
3. **The Cappadocian breakthrough (360s–370s):** Basil of Caesarea, Gregory of Nyssa, and Gregory of Nazianzus clarified "one ousia, three hypostaseis" — solving the confusion that had made homoousios seem like modalism
4. **Theodosius I (380):** New emperor, new policy — made Nicene faith the official state religion
5. **Constantinople I (381):** Finalized the Nicene-Cappadocian formula and condemned all anti-Nicene positions

### Key Figure: Constantius II (Sole Emperor 353–361)
The most important figure in the anti-Nicene period was not a theologian but an emperor. Constantius II:
- Called councils (Arles, Milan, all Sirmium councils, Rimini, Seleucia, Constantinople 360)
- Exiled pro-Nicene bishops (Athanasius 5x, Pope Liberius, Hilary, Hosius)
- Used armed guards, detention, and threats to coerce conciliar decisions
- Made homoian Christianity the official state religion for 20 years
- Without him, the anti-Nicene movement would likely have collapsed in the 350s

### The Scorecard

| Position | Councils For | Councils Against |
|---|---|---|
| Homoousios ("three persons, one God") | Nicaea 325, Alexandria 362, Constantinople 381 | Antioch 341, Arles 353, Milan 355, Sirmium II-V, Rimini 359, Seleucia 359, Constantinople 360 |
| Homoiousios ("similar substance") | Ancyra 358, Seleucia 359 (initially) | Sirmium III (357), Rimini 359, Constantinople 360 |
| Homoian ("just like") | Rimini 359, Seleucia 359, Constantinople 360 | Nicaea 325, Constantinople 381 |

**The uncomfortable fact:** The Nicene position that most Christians now consider "orthodox" was affirmed by 3 councils and condemned by ~10 in the 4th century. It won not through consensus but through the Cappadocian theological breakthrough, a new emperor, and one decisive council.

## SECTION 2: MAJOR REGIONAL & DENOMINATIONAL COUNCILS

### Council of Carthage (418 AD)
- **Heresy condemned:** Pelagianism
- **Plain language:** Humans are born innocent (no original sin), have full free will, and can achieve salvation through moral effort without needing God's grace
- **Condemned figure:** Pelagius, Celestius
- **Council's position:** Original sin is real, grace is necessary, humans cannot save themselves
- **Modern trap:** "God helps those who help themselves" is Pelagianism in a nutshell. If you think people are "basically good" and just need to "try harder" — that's Pelagius talking.

### Council of Orange (529 AD)
- **Heresy condemned:** Semi-Pelagianism
- **Plain language:** OK, humans are fallen, but they retain enough goodness to make the *first step* toward God on their own. Grace kicks in after human initiative.
- **Council's position:** Even the *desire* to believe comes from God's grace. Humans cannot initiate faith.
- **Modern trap:** "I decided to follow Jesus" / "I gave my life to Christ" / "I chose God" — this language, ubiquitous in evangelical altar calls, implies the human makes the first move. That's Semi-Pelagianism. Orange says grace comes first.

### Council of Trent (1545-1563)
- **Heresies condemned:** Protestant doctrines, each with formal "anathema sit"
  - Justification by faith alone → anathema
  - Scripture alone as authority → anathema
  - Only 2 sacraments (not 7) → anathema
  - Denial of transubstantiation → anathema
  - Rejection of purgatory → anathema
  - Denial of papal authority → anathema
- **Modern impact:** Every Protestant is formally under anathema from Trent. These were never officially rescinded (though Vatican II adopted a more irenic tone).

### Synod of Dort (1618-1619)
- **Heresy condemned:** Arminianism / Remonstrance
- **Plain language:** God's election is based on foreseen faith. Christ died for all people (not just the elect). Grace can be resisted. Believers can lose their salvation.
- **Condemned figures:** Followers of Jacob Arminius; 200 Arminian pastors defrocked, some exiled
- **Dort's position (Calvinist):** TULIP — Total Depravity, Unconditional Election, Limited Atonement, Irresistible Grace, Perseverance of the Saints
- **Modern trap:** Most Methodists, many Baptists, most Pentecostals, and the majority of global Christians hold Arminian views. Dort says they're wrong.

### Westminster Assembly (1643-1649)
- **Produced:** Westminster Confession of Faith — the doctrinal standard for Presbyterian and Reformed churches
- **Key positions:** Double predestination, covenant theology, regulative principle of worship, Sabbatarianism
- **Modern trap:** If you don't believe God predestined some people to hell *before creation* (double predestination), Westminster says your theology is deficient.

## SECTION 3: HERESIES MODERN CHRISTIANS UNKNOWINGLY HOLD

### Modalism / Sabellianism
- **The heresy:** God is one person who wears three "masks" — sometimes appearing as Father, sometimes as Son, sometimes as Spirit. NOT three distinct persons.
- **Condemned:** By Tertullian (~210 AD), and effectively at Nicaea (325) and Constantinople (381)
- **Modern prevalence:** EXTREMELY common. The "water/ice/steam" analogy, the "God wears different hats" explanation, the "one man who is a father, son, and husband" illustration — all modalism. Oneness Pentecostals hold explicit modalism.
- **Quiz angle:** "If God is like water that can be ice, liquid, or steam..." → Congratulations, you're a Sabellian.

### Docetism
- **The heresy:** Jesus only *appeared* to be human. His body was an illusion or phantom. He didn't really suffer or die.
- **Condemned:** Broadly from earliest church; 1 John 4:2 is likely anti-Docetist
- **Modern prevalence:** Any theology that makes Jesus's hunger, tiredness, fear, anger, or death seem like divine theater rather than genuine experience. Prosperity gospel's "Jesus was actually rich" claims.
- **Quiz angle:** "When Jesus cried at Lazarus's tomb, was he genuinely grieving or performing for our benefit?"

### Marcionism
- **The heresy:** The OT God is a different, inferior deity from Jesus's Father. The OT should be discarded.
- **Condemned:** Marcion expelled from Rome in 144 AD
- **Modern prevalence:** Churches that never preach the OT. Christians who say "that's Old Testament" to dismiss commands. The idea that the OT God is "wrathful" while Jesus is "loving."
- **Quiz angle:** "Is the God who commanded the conquest of Canaan the same God who said 'love your enemies'?"

### Patripassianism
- **The heresy:** God the Father suffered and died on the cross (because Father and Son aren't distinct persons)
- **Condemned:** By Tertullian, Origen, Athanasius (2nd-3rd century)
- **Modern prevalence:** Worship songs and prayers that blur Father and Son: "God died on the cross for me" (technically, God the *Son* died)
- **Quiz angle:** "Who was Jesus crying out to when he said 'My God, my God, why have you forsaken me'?"

### Montanism
- **The heresy:** The Holy Spirit gives ongoing binding revelation through modern prophets that supplements or supersedes Scripture
- **Condemned:** ~177 AD by synod in Asia Minor
- **Modern prevalence:** Charismatic churches where "the Lord told me..." carries the authority of Scripture. Prophetic movements with unaccountable leaders.
- **Quiz angle:** "Can God speak to you directly today with new revelation that has the same authority as Scripture?"

### The Great Exchange of Anathemas

| If you believe... | Condemned by... |
|---|---|
| Justification by faith alone | Council of Trent (Catholic) |
| Justification requires works | Protestant confessions |
| Unconditional election | Arminians |

- The 1529 Diet of Speyer declared: "Every Anabaptist... shall be condemned and brought from natural life into death by fire, sword, and the like."

- Felix Manz: drowned by Zwingli's Reformed Zurich (1527)
- Michael Sattler: burned by Catholics (1527)

## SECTION 5: MODERN CONTROVERSIES

### Open Theism
- **Claim:** God doesn't exhaustively know the future because free choices aren't determined yet
- **Condemned by:** Near-expulsion from the Evangelical Theological Society (2001, vote narrowly failed)
- **Who holds it:** Clark Pinnock, John Sanders, Greg Boyd
- **Why ordinary Christians may agree:** "Does God know what I'll have for breakfast tomorrow?" If you hesitate, you might be an Open Theist.

### Eternal Subordination of the Son (ESS/EFS)
- **Claim:** The Son eternally submits to the Father in role/function (even though equal in essence)
- **Condemned by:** ETS declared it doesn't conform to their doctrinal basis (2016); critics call it neo-subordinationism/Arianism
- **Who holds it:** Wayne Grudem, Bruce Ware (used to ground complementarianism)
- **Why it matters:** If you believe "the Son obeys the Father" is an eternal feature of the Trinity (not just during the incarnation), some theologians say you've revived Arianism.

### New Perspective on Paul
- **Claim:** "Works of the law" Paul opposed weren't works-righteousness but Jewish identity markers. Justification is about covenant membership, not individual salvation mechanics.
- **Condemned by:** Many Reformed theologians; John Piper's "The Future of Justification" was a direct rebuttal of N.T. Wright
- **Who holds it:** N.T. Wright, James D.G. Dunn
- **Why ordinary Christians may agree:** If you think Paul cared more about Jew-Gentile unity than about individual "getting saved" formulas, you're NPP-adjacent.

### Annihilationism / Conditional Immortality
- **Claim:** The unsaved are destroyed/cease to exist rather than suffering eternally in hell
- **Historical status:** Condemned by the Fifth Lateran Council (1513); rejected by most Protestant confessions
- **Who holds it:** John Stott, Edward Fudge, growing minority of evangelicals
- **Why ordinary Christians may agree:** If "eternal conscious torment" feels disproportionate or unjust to you, you're leaning annihilationist.

### Inclusivism
- **Claim:** People who never hear the gospel can still be saved through Christ without explicit faith in him
- **Condemned by:** Most evangelical institutions as undermining the Great Commission
- **Who holds it:** C.S. Lewis (arguably, in "The Last Battle"), Karl Rahner ("anonymous Christians"), Clark Pinnock
- **Why ordinary Christians may agree:** "What about people in the Amazon who never heard of Jesus?" If you think a just God wouldn't condemn them, you're inclusivist.

### Women in Ministry
- **Complementarian claim:** Women cannot hold teaching/authority positions over men
- **Egalitarian claim:** Galatians 3:28 dissolves gender hierarchy in the church
- **Who condemns whom:** Complementarians say egalitarians reject biblical authority; egalitarians say complementarians misread Paul's context
- **Why it matters:** Your position on 1 Timothy 2:12 puts you in one heretical camp or another depending on who you ask.

### Young Earth vs. Old Earth
- **YEC claim:** Earth is 6,000-10,000 years old; Genesis 1 is literal 24-hour days
- **OEC claim:** "Day" in Genesis can mean "age"; earth is 4.5 billion years old
- **Who condemns whom:** Ken Ham says OEC compromises Scripture; OEC says YEC misreads genre
- **Why ordinary Christians may be trapped:** No matter which you pick, someone calls you a heretic or a compromiser.

### Cessationism vs. Continuationism
- **Cessationist claim:** Miraculous gifts (tongues, prophecy, healing) ceased with the apostles
- **Continuationist claim:** All gifts continue today
- **Who condemns whom:** John MacArthur's "Strange Fire" conference explicitly called continuationism dangerous and borderline heretical; Charismatics say cessationists "quench the Spirit"
- **Quiz angle:** "Do you believe God still heals miraculously today?" — You're a heretic either way.

## SECTION 6: ORTHODOXY SHIFTS — KEY EXAMPLES

### Origen (185-254 AD)
- **Phase 1:** Greatest theologian of his era, universally respected
- **Phase 2:** Condemned posthumously at Constantinople II (553) — his views on pre-existing souls, universal salvation, and subordination of the Son were anathematized
- **Phase 3:** Modern rehabilitation — Pope Leo XIII praised him in 1893
- **Lesson:** The same person's theology can be orthodox, then heretical, then praiseworthy again

### Premillennialism
- **Phase 1:** Common early church view (Papias, Irenaeus, Justin Martyr, Tertullian)
- **Phase 2:** Rejected after Augustine; Augsburg Confession (1530) explicitly condemned it
- **Phase 3:** Revived in 19th century; now dominant in American evangelicalism (Left Behind, etc.)
- **Lesson:** A view can be mainstream, then heretical, then mainstream again

### Usury (Charging Interest)
- **Phase 1:** Condemned as sin — Second Lateran Council (1139), Council of Vienne (1311) declared defending usury to be heresy
- **Phase 2:** Gradually accepted with rise of capitalism
- **Phase 3:** Now completely normal; every Christian with a savings account benefits from usury
- **Lesson:** Economic realities overrode theological condemnation

### Slavery
- **Phase 1:** Defended theologically for centuries using Genesis 9, Leviticus slave laws, Pauline "obey your masters"
- **Phase 2:** Abolished; now universally condemned as evil
- **Lesson:** The "plain reading of Scripture" was used to defend slavery; other biblical principles eventually overturned it

### Galileo and Heliocentrism
- **Phase 1:** Galileo condemned by the Inquisition (1633) for heliocentrism
- **Phase 2:** Catholic Church formally acknowledged error in 1992 (359 years later)
- **Lesson:** Scientific consensus and theological "orthodoxy" can collide, and theology eventually adjusts

## SECTION 7: THE NT AUTHORS' ACTUAL PRIORITIES

*This section supports the website's concluding argument: rather than chasing "orthodoxy," focus on what the NT authors actually emphasized.*

### What the NT Authors Wrote Most About
1. **Love** — the supreme ethic (1 Cor 13, John 13:34-35, 1 John 4:7-21, Romans 13:8-10)
2. **Justice and care for the vulnerable** — widows, orphans, poor, strangers (James 1:27, Matthew 25:31-46, Luke 4:18-19)
3. **Unity and reconciliation** — Jew and Gentile, slave and free (Ephesians 2, Galatians 3:28, John 17)
4. **The resurrection of Jesus** — the central claim (1 Cor 15, Romans 1:4, Acts 2:24-32)
5. **Faithfulness and endurance** — not doctrinal precision but lived trust (Hebrews 11, James 2:14-26)
6. **Humility and servanthood** — Philippians 2:1-11, Mark 10:42-45

### What the NT Authors Did NOT Write About
- The precise metaphysics of the Trinity (the term "Trinity" never appears)
- The exact mechanism of atonement (multiple metaphors are used, never systematized)
- Predestination vs. free will as a binary system
- The age of the earth
- Whether spiritual gifts would cease
- Detailed eschatological timelines

### The Key Insight
The councils that defined "orthodoxy" were addressing important questions — but they were operating in Greek philosophical categories (ousia, hypostasis, physis) that the NT authors never used. The shift from "Jesus is Lord" (a relational, covenantal claim) to "Christ is homoousios with the Father" (a metaphysical claim) represents a genuine development — and it's a development that every subsequent "heresy" debate inherits.
